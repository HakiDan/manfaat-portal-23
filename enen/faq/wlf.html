Introduction

  • The Women's Leadership Foundation (WLF) aims to develop 2,500 future women leaders by 2025 in line with the national aspirations of the women's leadership agenda
  • WLF operates with a focus on three main pillars, namely, leadership, entrepreneurship and welfare

    • WLF’s Main Pillars Explanation on the programs
    Leadership
    • Through a memorandum of understanding (MoU) between WLF and the Asia School of Business (ASB), in collaboration with the MIT Sloan School of Management, a strategic joint venture through 'Women Leadership Accredited Certificate Programme' was held for for women leaders in Malaysia
    • Additionally, the SMART Sponsorship Journey program was also held, consisting of:
      1. Mentor Sponsorship Program (Mentorship-Sponsorship);
      2. A Round Table Session;
      3. ‘Talk Series’ sessions and;
      4. Experiential Industries Program
    • WLF has also organized the Sarawak Bootcamp Program 'Sarawak Women of Tomorrow 2022 (March 2022) and the Sabah Bootcamp Program (3rd Quarter 2022)
    Entrepreneurship
    • Young Aspiring Leaders & Entrepreneurs Program (YALE) is a milestone program for the pillar of entrepreneurship.
      1. This YALE program also fosters entrepreneurial spirit among unmet and B40 groups.
    • WLF also organizes a brainstorming session (Brainstorming Session ) with the National Federation of Women Entrepreneurs Association of Malaysia (FWEA)
    • WLF also organizes several forums and seminars physically and virtually through a joint venture with the Institute of Marketing Malaysia (IMM), Ernst & Young, Lembaga Tabung Haji, Bank Islam and the National University of Malaysia
    Welfare
    • WLF is also involved with the ‘Bakul Rakyat’ Program under UPEN of the Selangor State and 1,500 beneficiaries have received this assistance
      1. The Welfare Program organized by the WLF also emphasises the aspect of women's empowerment from an Islamic perspective
    • From the perspective of corporate social responsibility (CSR), WLF is also involved in providing assistance to target groups in need such as orphans, the B40 group, critical flood victims who suffered property damage during the severe floods that hit the country in December 2021

  • The goal of WLF is to create a 'critical mass' of trained women, whose potential has been honed ready to lead the leadership of public listed companies (PLCs), Government Linked Companies (GLCs), Government Linked Investment Companies (GLICs), public universities, public sector and institutions across every economic and social sector
    1. As of today, through the initiatives implemented by WLF, and through recommendations given to corporate companies, a total of 34 women leaders have been nominated as members of the Board of Directors.
  • For further information, please refer to the WLF Website
