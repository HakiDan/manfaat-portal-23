Independent Living Center (ILC)

No Applications Required

Last updated on 7/10/2022

Introduction

  • Independent Living Center (ILC) is one of the basic services offered by OKU groups to empower OKU.
  • In addition to focusing on the aspect of self -goal setting, Independent Living skills are provided so that OKUs are more prepared and sensitive to their needs to start life independently.
  • Objective of ILC:
    1. ILC is a Center managed by OKU for OKU with the aim of helping OKU live independently in society.
    2. To be a source of information for the disabled to obtain all information and services oriented on disability issues.
    3. To be a platform for the disabled to regain skills and self -confidence to return to society.
    4. To encourage and accelerate the development process for the disabled in terms of empowerment and enablement with the full participation of the disabled through advocacy programs.
    5. To be a center of reference for the disabled for all government and private parties.
  • Services provided by ILC:
    1. The basic services that is provided by ILC cover all categories of OKUs as follows:-
      1. Peer Counseling
      2. Independent Living Program
      3. Personal Assistant (PA) Services
    2. It should be noted that ILC is NOT a center of the following:
      1. Vocational Training Center for the Disabled; or
      2. Provide permanent residential services to the disabled.
  • However, there are temporary facilities provided for the success of the ILC program such as accommodation.
  • In addition, the ILC can also provide other support services and appropriate needs to the disabled such as transportation.
  • There is no fix period to the services provided, depending on the services required and the level of disability of the disabled.

Eligibility

  • There are no specific conditions and open to all categories of OKUs
