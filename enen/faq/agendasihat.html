Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS)

Open for Application

Last updated on 14/09/2022

Introduction

  • The core of ANMS is:
    1. Promotion of Healthy Living Culture
      • Foster a culture of healthy living by improving people's health literacy. Information is provided to improve health literacy and enable citizens to take responsibility for cultivating healthy living
      • Cultivating healthy living can strengthen self -resilience (self -immunity) to prevent disease
    2. Health Wellness Services
      • Facilitate the people access to health and wellness facilities and services. Facilities and services are provided for the benefit of the people to improve the health of the Rakyat
    3. Self Health Control
      • Rakyat are empowered in self -care for disease prevention. Rakyat are responsible for maintaining a healthy personal, family, community and environment
    4. Environmental Hygiene
      • • Foster a culture of environmental hygiene for the well -being and health of the people. The people are jointly responsible for maintaining the cleanliness of the environment to ensure a clean and disease-free environment
  • There are four objectives of ANMS as follows:
    1. Promoting healthy living culture to improve health well-being
    2. Provide an environment that supports well-being and health
    3. Empowering individuals and communities in self-health control
    4. Strengthen collaboration among stakeholders to achieve health well-being
  • Health is made a priority in all government/agency development plans (Health in All Policy) through the Whole of Nation approach as follows:
    1. The government has the responsibility to formulate the direction and strategy for the success of the ANMS. Policies, promotions and initiatives implemented must involve all parties to produce the desired impact
    2. The private sector plays a role in assisting programs planned by the government and should be able to combine existing services with government services for the benefit of the Rakyat
    3. Non-Governmental Organizations or NGOs have a role to play in supporting the ANMS by providing additional support in identified areas. NGOs can also act to lead implementation in the community with the cooperation of all parties
    4. The Rakyat can play a role by mobilizing efforts led by any party by engaging in the initiatives introduced in the ANMS
  • ANMS is implemented in phases over 10 years. The determination of the phase is dependent on current health issues
  • The performance of ANMS from the ministry is monitored twice a year. All ministries will submit achievement reports using the form provided
    1. Meanwhile, ANMS assessment will be carried out periodically to measure the achievements of ANMS in ensuring the health and quality of life of the Rakyat is achieved.

Eligibility

  • Malaysian Citizen
  • Below age 55
  • Wife of Head of Household (HOH)/Female HOH
  • Housewives who are registered in the National Database on Poverty (eKasih) as of November 2021
  • Must contribute a minimum of RM5 per month or RM60 per annum into your EPF account
  • Registered EPF Member
  • Opted to contribute under Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS)
    1. Submitted Form KWSP 16G(M)
  • Important Reminder: Housewives are only allowed to opt for one (1) incentive at a time. If you have registered for i-Saraan earlier, you would need to cancel it before reapplying for Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS). However this is depending on your eligibility.

Application Method

  • To apply for Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS):
    1. Complete the Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS) Form KWSP 16G(M)
    2. Register at any EPF Counters
  • Payments can be made through:
    1. Internet banking through Maybank, RHB Bank, Public Bank, Bank Muamalat, Alliance Bank, Hong Leong Bank, Bank Islam, CIMB Bank dan MBSB Bank
    2. Bank Agent Counters (cash / cheque) through BSN, Maybank, Public Bank, RHB Bank
    3. Registered BSN Bank Agents
    4. Any EPF payment counters using the KWSP 6A (2)
      • For further information please refer to List of EPF offices and kiosks
  • No application period
  • Documents required:
    1. MyKad
    2. Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS) Form -KWSP 16G(M) (Only for first time registration)
    3. Agenda Nasional Malaysia Sihat (ANMS) Form - KWSP 6A (2) (During payment)
    4. Payment in cash and cheque or through internet banking (form not required)
