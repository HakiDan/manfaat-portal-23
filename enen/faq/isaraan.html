i-Saraan

Open for Application

Last updated on 07/10/2022

Introduction

  • Voluntary Contribution with Retirement Incentive (i-Saraan) allows EPF members who are self-employed and do not earn a regular income to make voluntary contributions towards their retirement, and at the same time receive additional contribution as an incentive from the government.
  • Members aged below 60 years old will receive a 15% government incentive of the contribution made by the members, subject to a maximum of RM250/year.
  • i-Saraan benefits also include:
    1. A retirement avenue for those who are self-employed or do not earn a regular income
    2. Contribute at any amount, at any time according to your financial ability
    3. Can make withdrawals according to the conditions that has been determined
    4. Enjoy an annual dividend on your retirement savings
    5. Tax income exemption
    6. Death Benefit: RM2,500 (subject to EPF terms and conditions)

Eligibility

  • Malaysian Citizen
  • Below 60 years of age
  • Self-employed individuals and those who do not have a fix income
  • Self-employed individuals who derive income from own work and are not an employee
  • Self-employed individuals who does not have a fix income include the housewives
  • Registered EPF Member
  • Opted to contribute under i-Saraan
  • Submitted Form KWSP 16G(M)
  • For further information, please visit the i-Saraan Website

    • *Important Reminder: Housewives are only allowed to opt for one (1) incentive at a time. If you have registered for Insentif Suri (Kasih Suri Keluarga Malaysia KWSP) earlier, you would need to cancel it before reapplying for i-Saraan. However this is depending on your eligibility.

    Application Method

    • To register for i-Saraan:
      1. Complete the i-Saraan Form KWSP 16G(M)
      2. Apply online through the i-Saraan Registration Portal or EPF Counters
    • Payments can be made through:
      1. Internet banking through Maybank, RHB Bank, Public Bank, Bank Muamalat, Alliance Bank, Hong Leong Bank, Bank Islam, CIMB Bank and MBSB Bank
      2. Bank Agent Counters (cash / cheque) through BSN, Maybank, Public Bank, RHB Bank
      3. Registered BSN Bank Agent
      4. Any EPF payment counters using the KWSP 6A (2)
      5. For further information please refer to List of EPF offices and kiosks
    • Documents required:
      1. MyKad
      2. i-Saraan Form- KWSP 16G (M) (Only for first time registration)
      3. i-Saraan Form- KWSP 6A (2) (During payment)
      4. Payment in cash and cheque or through internet banking (form not required)
