Introduction

  • PEDi functions as a One Stop Centre to:
    1. Provide internet access to the community and areas with limited internet access.
    2. Offer ICT courses and training to the local community, encourage their participation in e-commerce and programs to increase their digital knowledge and skills.
    3. Promote awareness and empower the rural and urban poor community to uplift their socioeconomic status.
    4. Guide and equip small business entrepreneurs with the skills to use ICT to foster digital transformation in their business.
  • Various entrepreneurship courses are offered by government agencies including MDEC, HRDF, MEDAC, SME Corp as well as private companies such as AEON and Shopee. Examples of courses conducted at each PEDi are as follows:
    1. ICT training
    2. Entrepreneurship
    3. Multimedia training
    4. e-learning
    5. Marketing, business development, packaging, and other modules relevant to small businesses, and
    6. Awareness programs to encourage community participation in digital economy.
  • Digital transformation is PEDi’s main agenda to empower the rural and urban poor community so they too can participate in digital economic activities.

Eligibility

  • The eligibility may vary based on the requirements set by each participating agencies which organized their entrepreneurship programmes under this initiative.
