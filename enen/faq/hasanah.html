Hasanah Special Grant 2022

Applications Closed

Last updated on 07/10/2022

Introduction

  • The overall intent is to create real and lasting socio-economic and environmental change for Malaysia, through solutions that empower communities, encourage social inclusiveness, and improve the environment.
  • The initiative will support the works by civil society organisations on the ground as the country progresses towards the endemic phase, through programmes targeted at marginalised and/or vulnerable, including those in the Bottom 50 (B50), e.g., orang asal (indigenous), the elderly, the homeless, refugees, stateless and undocumented migrants, at-risk children, women and youth, and persons with disabilities or people with special needs from Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, and Sarawak.
  • The initiative will support the works by civil society organisations through grants to build resilience, both in natural ecosystems and in its people coping with recovering from Covid-19 in the areas of education, community development, environment and Arts and Public Spaces as described below:
    1. Education
      • Enhancing school leadership and maximizing student potential
      • Building Mental Health (MH) system capacity and raising awareness
      • Providing continuous educational support to students and teachers due to the impact caused by the pandemic
    2. Community Development
      • Supporting and empowering identified communities that will see a sustained improvement in income and wellbeing through the Hasanah Community Based Approach (CBA) model
      • Developing and enhancing community leadership (develop leaders who can self-organise and lead their communities in undertaking collective efforts to improve their quality of life)
      • Empowering communities through livelihood programmes
      • Improving the well-being of the community with the focus on mental health due to the impact caused by the pandemic
    3. Environment
      • Perak Sustainability – Focusing on restoration, conservation and protection of selected critical ecosystem linkages, empowering communities’ programmes, and collaboration with stakeholders
      • Plastics – Focusing on initiatives that reduces plastic waste in selected communities, schools, and workplaces
      • Building and enhancing conservation efforts for co-management of natural ecosystems and long-term monitoring of biodiversity, awareness and capacity building opportunities for youth and community participation on environmental conservation, climate readiness and animal welfare programmes nationwide.
    4. Arts and Public Spaces
      • Preservation and conservation of “endangered” textiles, creating an outreach and economic platform and copyrights
      • Continuous support for Arts and artisans due to the impact caused by the pandemic
  • Other considerations include scalability and replicability of the programme, opportunities to advocate for policy change for the better, how its impact can transform or catalyse further progress in the affected issue or area, as well as the sustainability of the programme.

Eligibility

Civil Society Organisations including:

  • Non-governmental Organisations (NGOs) that are not for profit, are registered under Registrar of Societies (ROS) for more than two (2) years and have audited accounts for the past two (2) years, or copies annual report for the last two (2) years submitted to Registrar of Societies (ROS)
  • Non-governmental organisations (NGOs) that are not for profit, established under the Trustees Act for more than two (2) years and with 2 years of audited financial accounts
  • Social Enterprises that are registered under Registrar of Companies (ROC) (e.g., Sdn Bhd or LLP or LLC), have attained MAGIC accreditation (either Basic, Accredited or Accredited Plus) with 2 years of income tax submission to Inland Revenue Board (IRB).

Application Method

  • The Hasanah Special Grant 2022 opened to receive application on 17 January 2022 to 30 June 2022 at the Hasanah Grant Portal
  • Eligibility of applicants will be assessed until 21 June 2022 and the portal closes for applications submission by eligible applicants on 30 June 2022.
  • Documents required:
    1. NGO under ROS
      • Certificate of Registration for non-profit NGOs
      • Audited financial accounts for the most recent 2 years.
    2. NGOs under Trustees Act
      • Certificate for Incorporation
      • 2 years of past audited financial accounts
    3. Social Enterprises (SE)
      • Certificate of Registration from Registrar of Companies (ROC)
      • Accreditation by MAGIC
      • Copies of 2 years income tax submission to Inland Revenue Board (IRB)
  • Grant application process starts with an Eligibility Assessment, which will require the applicants to fill and submit the associated documents on their eligibility.
  • Please click HERE to get started with Eligibility Assessment
  • Once eligible, applicants will receive a link to fill in the application form (online/web based) on Hasanah Grant Portal and upload the following documents as attachment:
    1. Implementation plan
    2. Detailed breakdown of funding requested
    3. Other documents as specified in the application form
