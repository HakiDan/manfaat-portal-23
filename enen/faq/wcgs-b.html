Working Capital Guarantee Scheme - Bumiputera (WCGS-B)

Open for Application

Last updated on 07/10/2022

Introduction

  • Working Capital Guarantee Scheme - Bumiputera (WCGS-B) is a government guarantee scheme to support Bumiputera SMEs to gain access to credit facilities from participating Financial Institutions required for their growth and high performance.
  • SMEs can apply for credit or financing facilities at our partnering Financial Institutions and if eligible, the Financial Institutions will submit guarantee scheme applications directly to SJPP.
  • Terms of the scheme as follows:

    • Item Explanation
    Scheme Limit
    • RM3 billion
    Tenure of Guarantee
    • Up to fifteen (15) years or until 31st December 2035, whichever is earlier
    Purpose of Financing
    • Working Capital
    • Capital Expenditure
    • This guarantee scheme cannot be used to refinance existing facility granted by the same or other participating financial institutions
    Type of Facility
    • Term Loan/ Term Financing, Overdraft/ Cash Line-i, Trade Facilities and Commercial Card
    Financing Limit
    • Minimum: RM100,000 only
    • Maximum: RM3 million only
    Guarantee Coverage
    • 80% guarantee of the financing obtained from the participating FIs (including principal and interest/ profit) by the Government of Malaysia
    Guarantee Fee
    • 0.75% per annum payable upfront
    Source of Funds/ Financing Facility
    • From the participating financial institutions
    Interest/ Profit Rate:
    • Determined by participating financial institutions

Eligibility

SME Companies in ALL sectors or industries

  • Non-Manufacturing:
    1. Companies with sales turnover not exceeding RM20.0 million OR full-time employees not exceeding 75 workers
  • Manufacturing:
    1. Companies with sales turnover not exceeding RM50.0 million OR full-time employees not exceeding 200 workers
  • Excluding:
    1. Entities that are public-listed on the main board; and
    2. Subsidaries of:
      1. Public-listed companies on the main board;
      2. Multinational corporations (MNCs);
      3. Government-linked companies (GLCs);
      4. Syarikat Menteri Kewangan Diperbadankan (MKDs); and
      5. State-owned enterprises
  • Incorporated under:
    1. Registration of Business Act 1956
    2. Companies Act 1965 or 2016
    3. Limited Liability Partnership Act 2012
    4. Respective authorities or district offices in Sabah and Sarawak; or
    5. Respective statutory bodies for professional services providers
  • Pegangan Saham:
    1. At least 51% or more shares held by Bumiputera for WCGS-B

Application Method

  • SME (Borrowers):
    1. SMEs to apply financing facilities from participating FIs
    2. SJPP does not provide any financing or credit facilities
  • Financial Institutions (FIs) (Lenders):
    1. FIs will review credit applications
    2. In the event SMEs lack or do not have any collateral, FIs will submit scheme applications to SJPP directly
  • SJPP:
    1. SJPP will review scheme applications for approval and revert to FIs
    2. Any further conditions/ requirement will be communicated to the FIs, and subsequently to the SME
    3. Up to 1% guarantee fee will be applied for the guarantee coverage
