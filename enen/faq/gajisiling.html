Increment of the Wage Ceiling Limit

No Application Required

Last updated on 09/01/2022

Introduction

  • Effective 1st September 2022, PERKESO will enforce a new wage ceiling for contributions from RM4,000 to RM5,000 per month.
  • The contribution amount applicable to employees with a salary up to RM5,000 per month is stated in the Third Schedule, Act 4 and the Second Schedule, Act 800
  • For employees with a salary of exceeding RM5,000 per month, the contribution amount is capped at the wage ceiling of RM5,000
    1. Accordingly, the contribution payment for the month of September 2022 and the subsequent months shall be made by the employer according to the new wage ceiling
    2. An increase in the salary ceiling cap would raise the existing interest-benefit payment rate by up to 25.3%. Covering Local Workers and Foreign Workers will benefit from increased salary ceiling cap
  • The benefits are as follows:

    • Employees Social Security Act, 1969, (Act 4) Employment Insurance System Act 2017 (Act 800)
  • Temporary Disability Benefit (FHUS)
  • Permanent Disability Benefit (FHUK)
  • Dependents' Benefits (FOT)
  • Survivorship Pension
  • Invalidity Pension
  • Jobseeking Allowance (EMP)
  • Early Re-employment Allowance (EBSA)
  • Reduced Income Allowance (EPP)
    		•
  • This enforcement is implemented in line with the agency's ongoing efforts towards providing more comprehensive social protection to its contributors and dependents
  • For the purpose of PERKESO’s records and inspection activities, all employers are required to adjust the PERKESO contribution deduction for both Acts in the Employee's Statement / Salary Slip as follows:
    1. PERKESO Contribution – Act 4
    2. PERKESO Contribution – Act 800
  • Employers can apply for contribution payment through the folowing methods:
    1. Contribution payment through Online Portal (FPX)
    2. Online Banking
    3. List of banks appointed as collecting agents as follows:
      • Maybank Berhad
      • Public Bank Berhad
      • RHB Bank Berhad

Eligibility

  • Two (2) types of contributions involved under Act 4 are:
    1. FIRST TYPE involves contribution paid for an employee who has not attained the age of 60 to be covered under both schemes namely the Employment Disaster Scheme and the Disability Scheme; or
    2. SECOND TYPE involves contribution paid for an employee who has attained the age of 60 and an employee who first entered the scheme after attaining the age of 55 to be covered under the Employment Disaster Scheme only
  • Under Act 800, only employees aged 18 until 60 are required to contribute. However, employees who have never registered and contributed after reaching the age of 57 are not eligible to be contributed under Act 800
